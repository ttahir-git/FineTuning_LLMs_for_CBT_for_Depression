4. **Mindfulness and Grounding Techniques**: When you feel overwhelmed, try some mindfulness exercises or grounding techniques. For example, you can use a **5-4-3-2-1 Technique**:
   - **5**: Notice 5 things you can see.
   - **4**: Notice 4 things you can touch.
   - **3**: Notice 3 things you can hear.
   - **2**: Notice 2 things you can smell.
   - **1**: Notice 1 thing you can taste.

5. **Regular Breaks**: Take regular breaks to engage in activities that you find relaxing or enjoyable. This could be reading a book, listening to music, or even taking a short walk.

6. **Seek Support**: Consider joining a support group for caregivers. Sharing your experiences and challenges with others who understand can be incredibly therapeutic and provide you with valuable insights and strategies.

7. **Professional Help**: If you find that these feelings are becoming overwhelming, don't hesitate to seek help from a therapist or counselor. They can provide you with personalized guidance and strategies to cope with stress and guilt.
