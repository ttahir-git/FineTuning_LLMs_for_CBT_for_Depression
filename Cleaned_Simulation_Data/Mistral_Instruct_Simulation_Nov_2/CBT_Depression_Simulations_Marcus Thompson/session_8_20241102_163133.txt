
Session Summary:

1. Main topics discussed:
- Managing stress related to mother's progressing Alzheimer's
- Balancing multiple responsibilities (teaching, community work, co-parenting, caregiving)
- Developing effective coping strategies

2. Techniques/exercises introduced:
- Task prioritization and organization
- Setting boundaries and learning to say no
- Incorporating small self-care activities into daily routine
- Exploring support group options for caregivers

3. Helpful/important elements:
- Recognition that current approach of trying to do everything at once isn't sustainable
- Acknowledgment that saying no is difficult but necessary
- Understanding that self-care isn't selfish but essential

4. Homework/tasks assigned:
- Write down and prioritize all current responsibilities
- Identify tasks that can be delegated or rescheduled
- Begin incorporating small self-care activities (walks during lunch, morning meditation)
- Look into caregiver support groups

5. Insights/realizations:
- Need to be more realistic about what can be handled
- Importance of finding balance rather than trying to do everything
- Value of connecting with others in similar situations
- Recognition that small, manageable changes can make a significant difference
