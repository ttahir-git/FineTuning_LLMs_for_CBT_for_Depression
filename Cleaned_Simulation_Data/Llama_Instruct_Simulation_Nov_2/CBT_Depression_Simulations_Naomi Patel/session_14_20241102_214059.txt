1. **Start by acknowledging your strengths and accomplishments**: Begin the letter by reminding yourself of your strengths, skills, and accomplishments. This can help you cultivate a sense of self-worth and confidence.

3. **Reframe your difficulties with self-compassion**: Use phrases like "I'm struggling with..." or "I'm feeling overwhelmed with..." and then reframe these struggles as opportunities for growth, learning, and self-compassion. 4. **Practice radical acceptance**: Recognize that your struggles are a natural part of the human experience, and that it's okay to feel overwhelmed or struggling. Avoid self-criticism and judgment, and instead, focus on acceptance and compassion.
5. **End with kindness and reassurance**: Close the letter by offering yourself kindness, reassurance, and support. Use phrases like "I'm doing the best I can," "I'm worthy of love and care," or "I'm strong and capable."
