Cognitive reappraisal is a technique used in cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) to help individuals change the way they think about and process negative emotions. **Reframed thought:** "I made a mistake, but it's not a disaster. I'll communicate with my team and stakeholders to explain what happened and find a solution."

To use cognitive reappraisal effectively, try the following steps:

1. **Identify the negative thought:** Recognize when you're having a negative thought pattern, such as catastrophizing or all-or-nothing thinking.
2. **Challenge the thought:** Ask yourself questions like "Is this thought really true?" or "Is there another way to look at this situation?"
3. **Reframe the thought:** Replace the negative thought with a more balanced and constructive one, as illustrated in the examples above.
4. **Practice the new thought:** Repeat the reframed thought to yourself several times, so it becomes more automatic and ingrained.

Remember, cognitive reappraisal is not about ignoring or denying your emotions, but about changing the way you think about and process them. With practice, you can develop a more balanced and constructive mindset, which can help you cope with challenges and setbacks more effectively.
