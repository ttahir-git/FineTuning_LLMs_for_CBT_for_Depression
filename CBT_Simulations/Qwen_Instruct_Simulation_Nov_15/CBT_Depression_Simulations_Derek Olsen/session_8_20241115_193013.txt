### Guided Meditation
Guided meditation involves following a trained guide (either in person, via an audio recording, or through a video) who leads you through a series of visualizations, relaxation exercises, and calming affirmations. The goal is to help you enter a deep state of relaxation, which can reduce anxiety and promote mental clarity and emotional balance.

**How It Works:**
1. **Finding a Quiet Space:** You’ll need a quiet and comfortable place to sit or lie down.
2. **Following the Guide:** The guide will lead you through breathing exercises, visualizations, and positive affirmations.
3. **Relaxation Response:** As you follow the guide, you’ll enter a state of deep relaxation, which can help reduce stress hormones like cortisol.

### Body Scan Meditation
A body scan is a mindfulness practice that involves focusing your attention on different parts of your body, starting from your feet and moving up to your head. This practice helps you become more aware of physical sensations, tension, and areas where you might be holding stress.

**How It Works:**
1. **Lie Down Comfortably:** Find a quiet and comfortable place to lie down.
2. **Focus on Your Breath:** Take a few deep, slow breaths to help you relax.
3. **Scan Your Body:** Slowly move your attention from your toes to the top of your head, noticing any sensations, tensions, or discomfort in each part of your body.
4. **Release Tensions:** As you become aware of tension in a part of your body, try to consciously relax that area.

### Applying These Techniques
Let’s start by incorporating a brief guided meditation or body scan into your routine. You can try it once or twice a day, perhaps in the morning or before bed. Here’s a simple example of a body scan:

**Body Scan Example:**
1. **Lie Down Comfortably:** Find a quiet place to lie down.
2. **Focus on Your Breath:** Take a few deep, slow breaths.
3. **Start at Your Feet:** Bring your attention to your feet. Notice any sensations, tension, or relaxation.
4. **Move Upward:** Gradually move your attention up your legs, belly, chest, arms, and head.
5. **Release Tensions:** As you notice any tension, imagine it slowly releasing and leaving your body.
6. **Finish with Deep Breaths:** Finish by taking a few slow, deep breaths and notice how you feel.
